Networking & Building Connections

Build Your Professional Network

Networking is one of the most powerful tools for career success. Building authentic connections with professionals in your field can open doors to opportunities, provide valuable insights, and help you navigate your career path. Whether you're an introvert or extrovert, networking is a skill you can develop and master.

At UMSI, you have access to a vibrant alumni network, industry professionals, and fellow students who can support your career journey. This page provides you with the strategies, templates, and confidence to make meaningful connections that will benefit you throughout your career.

Why Networking Matters

  • Discover Hidden Opportunities: Many jobs and internships are filled through referrals and networking before they're ever posted publicly.
  • Gain Industry Insights: Learn about different career paths, company cultures, and industry trends directly from people working in the field.
  • Build Your Support System: Develop relationships with mentors, peers, and professionals who can offer advice and support.
  • Enhance Your Skills: Networking helps you practice communication, build confidence, and develop professional relationships.
  • Give Back: As you grow in your career, you'll have opportunities to help others just as people have helped you.

Getting Started with Networking

  1. Identify Your Goals: Determine what you want to learn or achieve through networking. Are you exploring career options, seeking a mentor, or looking for job opportunities?
  2. Start Close to Home: Begin with your classmates, professors, and UMSI alumni. These connections already have something in common with you.
  3. Prepare Your Introduction: Develop a brief, authentic way to introduce yourself and explain your interests and goals.
  4. Ask Thoughtful Questions: Show genuine interest in others' experiences and perspectives. People enjoy sharing their stories.
  5. Follow Up: After meeting someone, send a thank you message and stay in touch. Networking is about building lasting relationships.
  6. Give as Well as Receive: Think about how you can be helpful to others in your network, whether through sharing resources, making introductions, or offering your own expertise.

Networking Tips

How do I network if I'm introverted?
Networking doesn't have to mean large events. One-on-one coffee chats, informational interviews, and online connections through LinkedIn can be just as effective and may feel more comfortable.
What should I say when reaching out to someone I don't know?
Be genuine and specific. Mention how you found them, what interests you about their work, and what you hope to learn. Keep your initial message brief and respectful of their time.
How do I follow up after meeting someone?
Send a thank you email within 24-48 hours. Reference something specific from your conversation, express your appreciation, and suggest staying in touch.
Is it okay to ask for job referrals?
Build the relationship first. Focus on learning and connecting authentically. Once you've established a genuine connection, it's appropriate to ask for advice about opportunities or if they'd be willing to refer you.
How often should I reach out to my network?
Stay in touch periodically by sharing relevant articles, congratulating people on achievements, or checking in a few times a year. Quality matters more than quantity.
