Networking & Building Connections

Build Your Professional Network

Networking is one of the most powerful tools for career success. Building authentic connections with professionals in your field can open doors to opportunities, provide valuable insights, and help you navigate your career path.

At UMSI, you have access to a vibrant alumni network, industry professionals, and fellow students who can support your career journey.

Why Networking Matters

  • Discover Hidden Opportunities: Many jobs are filled through referrals before they're ever posted publicly.
  • Gain Industry Insights: Learn about different career paths and company cultures directly from people in the field.
  • Build Your Support System: Develop relationships with mentors, peers, and professionals who can offer advice.
  • Enhance Your Skills: Networking helps you practice communication and build confidence.
  • Give Back: As you grow, you'll have opportunities to help others just as people have helped you.

Getting Started with Networking

  1. Identify Your Goals: Determine what you want to achieve—exploring career options, finding a mentor, or discovering job opportunities.
  2. Start Close to Home: Begin with classmates, professors, and UMSI alumni. They already have something in common with you.
  3. Prepare Your Introduction: Develop a brief, authentic way to introduce yourself and explain your interests.
  4. Ask Thoughtful Questions: Show genuine interest in others' experiences. People enjoy sharing their stories.
  5. Follow Up: After meeting someone, send a thank you message and stay in touch.
  6. Give as Well as Receive: Think about how you can be helpful to others in your network too.

Networking Tips & FAQ

How do I network if I'm introverted?
Networking doesn't have to mean large events. One-on-one coffee chats, informational interviews, and online connections through LinkedIn can be just as effective.
What should I say when reaching out to someone I don't know?
Be genuine and specific. Mention how you found them, what interests you about their work, and what you hope to learn. Keep your message brief.
How do I follow up after meeting someone?
Send a thank you email within 24–48 hours. Reference something specific from your conversation and suggest staying in touch.
Is it okay to ask for job referrals?
Build the relationship first. Once you've established a genuine connection, it's appropriate to ask for advice about opportunities.
How often should I reach out to my network?
Stay in touch periodically by sharing relevant articles or congratulating people on achievements a few times a year.
