Planning Your Job & Internship Search

Navigate Your Job Search with Confidence

Searching for a job or internship can feel overwhelming, but with the right strategy and resources, you can approach your search with confidence and purpose.

UMSI students have access to exclusive resources, a supportive alumni network, and expert guidance from the Career Development Office.

Why Planning Your Search Matters

  • Save Time and Energy: A strategic approach helps you focus on opportunities that truly fit your goals.
  • Increase Your Success Rate: Targeted applications with tailored materials are more likely to result in interviews and offers.
  • Reduce Stress: Having a clear plan and timeline makes the search process more manageable.
  • Discover Better Opportunities: Thoughtful research helps you find roles and companies you might not have considered.

Steps to Plan Your Job & Internship Search

  1. Define Your Goals: Identify what you're looking for—industry, location, company size, and type of work.
  2. Research Potential Employers: Explore companies that align with your interests, mission, and culture.
  3. Create a Target List: Develop a list of 15–30 companies you'd like to work for and prioritize by fit.
  4. Develop Your Materials: Prepare a strong resume, cover letter templates, and portfolio if applicable.
  5. Set Up Your Search System: Use a spreadsheet to track applications, deadlines, contacts, and follow-ups.
  6. Leverage Multiple Strategies: Use job boards, networking, career fairs, and UMSI-specific resources.
  7. Create a Timeline: Set realistic weekly goals and build in time for research and customization.
  8. Prepare for the Long Game: Job searching takes time. Stay persistent through multiple rounds of applications.

Search Strategies for UMSI Students

Use UMSI-Specific Resources

Start with Career Link, which features opportunities specifically seeking UMSI students. Employers posting here already know your program and skills.

Network Your Way In

Many positions are filled through referrals. Connect with UMSI alumni at companies you're interested in. Informational interviews can lead to job opportunities.

Target Companies, Not Just Jobs

Identify companies doing interesting work even without current openings. Follow them, connect with employees, and apply when positions open.

Consider Multiple Pathways

UMSI skills apply to UX, data science, product management, information security, consulting, and more.

Apply Early and Often

Many companies recruit on academic calendars. Start your search early—summer internship deadlines often fall in the fall semester.

Job Search FAQ

When should I start my job or internship search?
For summer internships, start in late summer or early fall. For full-time positions, begin 6–9 months before your target start date.
How many applications should I submit?
Quality matters more than quantity. Ten well-researched, tailored applications outperform fifty generic ones.
What if I don't meet all the qualifications in a job posting?
If you meet 60–70% of qualifications and are genuinely interested, apply. Highlight how your skills transfer to areas where you fall short.
Should I apply to jobs that don't specifically mention my major?
Absolutely! UMSI students have diverse, transferable skills. Focus on roles matching your interests, not just those mentioning your degree.
How do I stay motivated during a long job search?
Set small, achievable weekly goals. Celebrate small wins. Connect with peers going through the same process.
What should I do while waiting to hear back from employers?
Continue applying, develop skills through projects or online courses, network, and prepare for potential interviews.
