Planning Your Job & Internship Search

Navigate Your Job Search with Confidence

Searching for a job or internship can feel overwhelming, but with the right strategy and resources, you can approach your search with confidence and purpose. Whether you're looking for your first internship, a summer position, or a full-time role after graduation, a well-planned search will help you find opportunities that align with your skills, interests, and career goals.

UMSI students have access to exclusive resources, a supportive alumni network, and expert guidance from the Career Development Office. This page will help you organize your search, identify opportunities, and stand out to employers in the competitive job market.

Why Planning Your Search Matters

  • Save Time and Energy: A strategic approach helps you focus on opportunities that truly fit your goals rather than applying randomly.
  • Increase Your Success Rate: Targeted applications with tailored materials are more likely to result in interviews and offers.
  • Reduce Stress: Having a clear plan and timeline makes the search process more manageable and less overwhelming.
  • Discover Better Opportunities: Thoughtful research helps you find roles and companies you might not have considered.
  • Build Valuable Skills: The search process itself helps you develop research, communication, and self-advocacy skills.

Steps to Plan Your Job & Internship Search

  1. Define Your Goals and Priorities: Identify what you're looking for in a role, including industry, location, company size, and type of work. Consider your must-haves and nice-to-haves.
  2. Research Potential Employers: Explore companies and organizations that align with your interests. Look into their mission, culture, products, and career opportunities.
  3. Create a Target List: Develop a list of 15-30 companies or organizations you'd like to work for. Prioritize them based on fit and interest.
  4. Develop Your Materials: Prepare a strong resume, cover letter templates, and portfolio if applicable. Tailor these materials for each application.
  5. Set Up Your Search System: Use a spreadsheet or tool to track applications, deadlines, contacts, and follow-ups. Stay organized throughout the process.
  6. Leverage Multiple Search Strategies: Use job boards, networking, company websites, career fairs, and UMSI-specific resources to find opportunities.
  7. Create a Timeline: Set realistic goals for applications per week and build in time for research, customization, and follow-up.
  8. Prepare for the Long Game: Job searching takes time. Plan for multiple rounds of applications and stay persistent.

Search Strategies for UMSI Students

Use UMSI-Specific Resources

Start with Career Link, which features opportunities specifically seeking UMSI students. Employers posting here are already familiar with your program and skills.

Network Your Way In

Many positions are filled through referrals. Connect with UMSI alumni working at companies you're interested in. Informational interviews can lead to job opportunities.

Target Companies, Not Just Jobs

Identify companies doing work you find interesting, even if they don't have current openings. Follow them, connect with their employees, and apply when positions open.

Consider Multiple Pathways

Look beyond traditional job titles. UMSI skills apply to roles in UX, data science, product management, information security, consulting, and more.

Apply Early and Often

Many companies recruit on academic calendars. Start your search early, especially for summer internships which often have fall deadlines.

Job Search FAQ

When should I start my job or internship search?
For summer internships, start in late summer or early fall. For full-time positions after graduation, begin 6-9 months before your target start date. Earlier is always better.
How many applications should I submit?
Quality matters more than quantity. It's better to submit 10 well-researched, tailored applications than 50 generic ones. Aim for a steady, consistent pace.
What if I don't meet all the qualifications in a job posting?
Job postings often describe an ideal candidate. If you meet 60-70% of the qualifications and are genuinely interested, apply. Highlight how your skills transfer to the requirements you don't fully meet.
Should I apply to jobs that don't specifically mention my major?
Absolutely! UMSI students have diverse, transferable skills. Focus on roles that match your skills and interests, not just those that mention your specific degree.
How do I stay motivated during a long job search?
Set small, achievable weekly goals. Celebrate small wins. Connect with peers going through the same process. Take breaks when needed. Remember that rejection is normal and doesn't reflect your worth.
What should I do while waiting to hear back from employers?
Continue applying to other positions, develop your skills through projects or online courses, network, and prepare for potential interviews. Don't put your search on hold.
